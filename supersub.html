02 Strategy

Not views.
The right views.

The goal was never to go viral. It was to build a content system where every post, every ad, and every landing page serves a specific role in the funnel — and the data tells us exactly which role is working.

The Philosophy
"1 million views from the wrong audience gives you 10 buyers. 10,000 views from the right people gives you 50. We optimize for the second scenario — every time."
The Problem

Supersub operates in construction — a high-consideration, high-ticket space ($7,900–$8,940 NZD per unit). Typical content plays don't work here. You can't entertain your way to a sale. Buyers need to trust the product, understand the specs, and believe the company delivers.

Traditional content agencies treat every brand the same. Supersub needed a system built around how construction buyers actually make decisions.

The Framework

Every piece of content is mapped to one of 5 content pillars before production begins: Educate (25%), Problem (15%), Solution (25%), Trust (20%), Convert (15%). The question before every script: "What is the purpose of this post?"

This applies across all channels — TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn — and is documented inside the custom Content Dashboard for the whole team to reference.

Content Architecture for Supersub
1
Define the content universe
Mapped out 12 content categories, 5 pillars, and 120+ topic ideas for each product line (Snaplock Wall, SIP Panel). Documented in the Content Dashboard — not in someone's head.
2
Produce to purpose
Every carousel, reel, and short-form video is scripted with a clear pillar objective. Carousels handle education and trust. Reels drive reach. Ads drive intent.
3
Measure and iterate
TikTok search traffic %, Facebook CPL, GSC click growth, GA4 generate_lead events — all tracked. Poor-performing angles are cut. Strong ones are scaled.
4
Build infrastructure to sustain it
Two custom dashboards, a CRM replacing HubSpot, and n8n automation — built so the system can scale without proportionally scaling headcount.
Key Differentiator
This is not a freelancer managing posts. It's an end-to-end growth system — from content strategy to lead capture to quote delivery — designed, built, and operated by one person.

Creative Approach

High-performing ads used clean JPEG product photography — no motion, no heavy editing. The product speaks for itself. The creative job was to stop the scroll and get the right person to raise their hand.

Testimonial variants and lifestyle angles were tested. Product-direct remained the strongest performer across all testing rounds.

Targeting & Audience

Targeting built around construction decision-makers: business owners, property developers, commercial builders in New Zealand. Interest and behavior layering refined over multiple campaign iterations.

Warm retargeting audiences built from website traffic and video viewers — fed by the organic social and SEO channels running in parallel.

The Shift That Matters

Traffic used to depend almost entirely on branded queries — people who already knew Supersub. That's not acquisition, it's retention.

The content strategy shifted toward non-branded, intent-based keywords: "SIP homes NZ," "modular home comparison," "snaplock wall cost." People who have never heard of Supersub but are actively searching for what Supersub sells.

Homepage position improvement from 37.23 → 15.19 in 6 months reflects this new keyword targeting — without link building.

Data-Informed Iteration

The Modular Comparison article recorded the highest average engagement time (142 seconds) of any page — readers finish it. But zero leads converted from it.

Diagnosis: the content is excellent, the CTA architecture is not. The next iteration targets this gap — introducing a direct path from article → quote request at the point of peak engagement.

This is what separates data-informed SEO from blog writing.
