03 My Role

From Brief
to Ready-to-Shoot

My job was to take the N'Pure brief and turn it into a production-ready creative document — something the director could open and immediately understand the look, feel, and shot plan for every product scene.

✍️
Script Writing
Wrote the full creative script for the 30-second vertical video — scene-by-scene narration and shot intent, aligned to N'Pure's brand voice and key message.
🔍
Reference Research
Sourced visual references from YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, and Pinterest — hunting for product cinematography and organic/nature aesthetics that matched the brief.
🗂️
Eagle Curation
Organized all references in Eagle — tagged by scene, product, and shot type. Built a shared visual library the entire team could pull from during production prep.
🎬
Timeline Moodboard
Assembled the curated references into a scene-by-scene timeline — mapping each shot type (wide, close-up, flying motion, overhead) to the product sequence.
🎨
Deck Design (PPT)
Designed the final Creative Deck in PowerPoint — visually aligned with N'Pure's organic identity, structured to present clearly and feel personal when shown to the brand.
📋
Client Presentation
Built the deck to be self-explanatory in presentation — each slide structured so the brand team could visualize the final video before a single camera was turned on.
Step 01
Receive Brief → Write Script
Reviewed N'Pure's brand guidelines, product specs, and key message. Drafted the script — defining scene intentions, pacing, and product focus across the 30-second runtime.
Step 02
Research Across Platforms
Combed through YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, and Pinterest for reference videos matching the organic/nature aesthetic — focusing on product cinematography with calming, clean visual language.
Step 03
Organize in Eagle
Imported and tagged every reference in Eagle by product, scene type, and shot angle — building a structured visual library the whole team could access during prep.
Step 04
Build Timeline Moodboard
Selected the strongest references for each scene and arranged them into a chronological moodboard timeline — wide shots, close-ups, flying motions, overhead angles — matching the script sequence.
Step 05
Design Deck → Present to Brand
Built the final Creative Deck in PowerPoint — clean, brand-consistent, and structured to align director and client before production day. No ambiguity on set.
